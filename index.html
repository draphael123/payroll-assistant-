Workflow

How It Works

The payroll cycle runs in three phases — ongoing tracking, a 48-hour pre-check, and the processing morning.

Part 1 — Throughout the Pay Period

Variance Tracker Setup & Ongoing Logging

At the start of each period, prepare the Variance Tracker sheet. Log licensing reimbursements as they occur. Record shift differentials. Notify Finance immediately on new hires, terminations, rate changes, or status changes.

Part 2 — 48 Hours Before Processing

Pre-Payroll Checklist

Export Gusto timesheets, time punch reports, and Google Calendar shifts. Final capture of shift diffs and reimbursements. Confirm Finance has been notified on all changes. Run DST check if the period crosses a transition date.

Part 3 — Payroll Processing Morning

Variance Scan → Approval → Correction

Open all systems side-by-side. Compare Calendar vs. Gusto vs. punches for each provider. Flag mismatches in the tracker. Send flags to Ashley/Brittany for approval. Get provider confirmations. Make approved corrections in Gusto. Prep summary for Lindsay meeting by 8:00 AM EST.

⚠
Key rule: Flag first, confirm second, change third. No Gusto edits until Ashley or Brittany approve.

Special Handling

DST & Timezone Rules

Daylight saving transitions cause a 1-hour discrepancy for MT/MST providers. Check every period that crosses a transition date.

Identify the transition date

Second Sunday in March (spring forward) or first Sunday in November (fall back).

Review affected providers

Note which shifts fell before vs. after the transition. Compare Gusto timezone vs. time punch timezone.

Confirm & adjust

If a 1-hour gap appears around 2:00 AM on transition day, confirm with the provider and adjust Gusto to reflect actual hours worked.

🌵
Arizona exception: AZ does not observe DST. Providers there are permanently on MST (UTC-7). During DST months (Mar–Nov), AZ is 1 hour behind MT states like Colorado and Montana.
